## 1. Purpose

Why this SOP exists.

- What problem this SOP solves  
- Why consistency matters  
- Business outcome protected or enabled  
- Risk mitigated by following this procedure  

Clarity of purpose prevents procedural drift.

---

## 2. Scope

Define where this SOP applies — and where it does not.

### Included
- Roles, teams, systems, or scenarios covered  

### Excluded
- Edge cases handled elsewhere  
- Explicit non-applicability  

Scope boundaries prevent authority overlap.

## 5. Prerequisites

What must be true before execution begins.

- Required access or permissions  
- Required tools or systems  
- Required training or certifications  
- Required inputs or dependencies  

Unverified prerequisites introduce failure risk.

---

## 6. Trigger & Frequency

### Trigger Events

When this SOP is initiated.

Examples:
- Contract signed  
- Invoice issued  
- System alert triggered  
- Scheduled review date  

### Frequency

- One-time  
- Daily  
- Weekly  
- Monthly  
- Quarterly  
- Ad-hoc  

Defined triggers prevent ambiguity.

---

## 7. Procedure

The execution playbook.

### 7.1 Step-by-Step Process

1. **Step Name**

   - Action description  
   - Responsible role  
   - Tools used  
   - Expected outcome  

2. **Step Name**

   - Action description  
   - Decision point (if yes/no, branch clearly)  

Repeat as required.

Steps must be unambiguous and testable.

---

### 7.2 Decision Points & Exceptions

Define how deviations are handled.

- If X occurs → do Y  
- If threshold Z is exceeded → escalate to [Role]  
- If prerequisite fails → pause execution  

Procedural flexibility must remain controlled.

---

## 8. Quality Standards & Acceptance Criteria

Define what “done correctly” means.

- Quality benchmarks  
- Review or approval requirements  
- Tolerance thresholds  
- Validation checks  

Without acceptance criteria, quality is subjective.

---

## 9. Outputs & Records

Define what artifacts are produced.

- Deliverables  
- Storage location  
- Naming conventions  
- Retention period  

Documentation is part of the control structure.

## 10. Controls & Risk Mitigation

- Preventive controls  
- Detective controls  
- Known risks  
- Mitigation strategies  
- Risk register reference (if applicable)  

Controls reduce systemic fragility.

---

## Closing Principle

An SOP is not documentation for documentation’s sake.

An SOP:

- Reduces ambiguity  
- Defines authority  
- Preserves consistency  
- Protects stability  
- Enables scale without fragility  

Document before delegating.  
Define before executing.  
Govern before expanding.
